### Phân đoạn 2.1: Số thực xấp xỉ, Scale và sai số (00:00 - 05:00)
- **Lời thoại AI (Audio Voiceover):**
  "In the previous scene, we followed a real vector into a noisy polynomial ciphertext. We now continue from that bridge and open the C-K-K-S representation itself. The first fact is that C-K-K-S preserves useful numerical meaning, not exact bit patterns. On the enlarged number line, the blue point is the original value of pi and the yellow point is the decoded result. They are extremely close, but they are not identical. The red arrow measures the small difference. What matters is whether the decoded point remains inside the green tolerance chosen by the application. For image features and neural-network scores, numerical closeness is often the correct notion of correctness.

  To carry decimal information into ring arithmetic, the encoder first multiplies the value by a large scale. Watch 3.14159265 move into the wheel marked two to the power of forty. The scale shifts meaningful fractional information into a large integer-like magnitude. The growing precision marks represent the detail being preserved before rounding. The token then passes through the round operation and becomes polynomial-compatible data. The scale does not make the representation exact, and it is not merely a display unit. It is metadata that explains how the stored magnitude should later be interpreted. Choosing it too small loses precision early; choosing it too large consumes modulus capacity too quickly.

  Decoding performs the reverse numerical interpretation. The stems on the left represent polynomial coefficients produced by the encoder. They are not a row of readable decimal values, yet together they carry the packed message. As the coefficients move through the divide-by-scale stage, the decoder reconstructs a slot value near 3.14159. The blue reference above it shows the original input. The short red distance between them is the residual approximation error. This visual is deliberately asymmetric: encoding and decoding are not simple text formatting. They map between numerical slots and a ring element, while the tracked scale connects the two domains. A successful round trip preserves the useful digits required by the application.

  Approximation error does not enter at only one point. Follow the white token through the complete path. Encoding rounds a scaled value into ring data. Encryption adds randomized noise required by security. Homomorphic evaluation changes both numerical values and ciphertext state. Rescaling divides by a modulus prime and introduces another controlled rounding. Each colored contribution extends the same error bar rather than replacing the previous contribution. Good C-K-K-S design therefore budgets error across the whole circuit. No single stage needs to be perfectly exact, but the accumulated effect must remain small enough that decryption and decoding still recover a meaningful numerical answer.

  The final test compares the encrypted computation with a plaintext baseline. The blue point marks the ordinary result and the red point marks the decrypted C-K-K-S result. First define the green tolerance band from the needs of the application. Then measure the absolute distance between the two points. Here the encrypted result remains inside the accepted region, so the test reports pass. A relative-error check may also be important when output magnitudes vary widely. The key lesson is that tolerance is not an excuse for arbitrary error. It is an explicit numerical contract. The circuit, scale, modulus chain, and validation procedure must all be chosen so the observed error satisfies that contract."

### Phân đoạn 2.2: Đóng gói SIMD và phép quay các Slot (05:00 - 10:00)
- **Lời thoại AI (Audio Voiceover):**
  "Now move from one approximate value to an entire packed vector. The upper row shows six independent input values. During encoding, each value moves straight into the slot directly below it, so the correspondence between input position and encoded position remains visible. The C-K-K-S encode label marks this numerical transform. The slots together form one polynomial representation; they are not six separately encrypted scalars. In the final step, the complete slot row moves into one red ciphertext container. One ciphertext can therefore carry the whole vector. This packing is central to practical C-K-K-S because one homomorphic instruction can be shared across every occupied slot. Slot layout must be planned as part of the algorithm, before encryption begins.

  The number of available complex slots is tied to the polynomial modulus degree N. As the selector moves from 4096 to 8192 and then 16384, the visible grid expands and the counter changes from 2048 to 4096 and then 8192 slots. In C-K-K-S, the standard packing capacity is N divided by two complex values. Larger N therefore creates more parallel capacity and can support a larger coefficient-modulus budget. But the expansion is not free. Ring operations become more expensive, ciphertexts use more memory, and key material grows. The middle setting, N equal to 8192, gives 4096 complex slots in the educational configuration used later in this scene.

  Packed arithmetic behaves like Single Instruction, Multiple Data. The blue row and the yellow row each contain four slot values. Both rows enter one addition gate. The gate is invoked once, yet the four output positions appear together as 1 plus 10, 2 plus 20, 3 plus 30, and 4 plus 40. Multiplication follows the same slot-wise pattern. No slot automatically reads a neighboring slot, and the cloud still sees only encrypted objects. This parallel behavior is why a careful packing layout can make private inference practical. One ciphertext operation can update many image features, channels, or examples at the same time instead of paying the full cryptographic cost for every scalar.

  Public plaintext masks let us shape packed encrypted data cheaply. The red row contains encrypted values, while the green row broadcasts the pattern one, zero, one, zero, one, zero. Multiplication by this mask leaves selected positions unchanged and suppresses the alternating positions. Watch the zero-selected cells fade before the yellow result appears. The server learns the public mask, but it does not learn the private values stored in the ciphertext. Similar masks can isolate channels, select lanes, or apply public weights. They are useful, but they do not move information between slots. They only transform each current position according to the plaintext value aligned with that position.

  To move information between positions, C-K-K-S uses rotations. In the first phase, the vector 1, 2, 3, 4 shifts right by one position and becomes 4, 1, 2, 3. The Galois key shown beneath the arrow enables the automorphism and key-switching step; it does not reveal the slot values. The first diagram then clears before the reduction begins. After one rotate-and-add stage, the partial sums are 5, 3, 5, 7. Rotating that partial vector by two positions gives 5, 7, 5, 3. Adding the two rows slot by slot produces 10 in every slot. This staged pattern builds packed sums, dot products, and convolution-like data movement without exposing the encrypted vector."

- **Hoạt ảnh Manim (Visual Actions):**
  1. Dùng ba tầng dọc tách biệt: `input values` ở trên, `encoded slots` ở giữa và `one packed ciphertext` ở dưới. Các giá trị bay thẳng xuống slot tương ứng; cung `CKKS encode` nhấn mạnh phép biến đổi trước khi cả hàng slot được đặt vào một ciphertext.
  2. Cho selector hình tam giác chạy bên dưới thanh `N = 4096 -> 8192 -> 16384`, còn nhãn N nằm phía trên nên không bị che. SlotGrid mở rộng và bộ đếm đổi `2048 -> 4096 -> 8192 complex slots`.
  3. Hai hàng packed vector cùng chảy vào một cổng cộng. Bốn output slot xuất hiện gần như đồng thời và được flash nối tiếp ngắn để chứng minh một lệnh xử lý toàn vector.
  4. Hàng mask `1,0,1,0,1,0` hạ xuống ciphertext. Những slot ứng với zero tự mờ đi, rồi hàng kết quả chỉ giữ các vị trí được chọn.
  5. Chia thành hai pha. Pha một chỉ minh họa `[1,2,3,4] -> rotate by 1 -> [4,1,2,3]`, với thẻ `Galois key` ghi rõ rằng key cho phép phép dịch slot. Sau đó xóa pha một; pha hai cộng `partial sums [5,3,5,7]` với bản quay hai vị trí `[5,7,5,3]` để tạo `[10,10,10,10]`.

### Phân đoạn 2.3: Polynomial Degree, Modulus Chain và Rescaling (10:00 - 15:00)
- **Lời thoại AI (Audio Voiceover):**
  "Packing capacity is only one effect of the polynomial modulus degree. As the selector moves to larger N, the slot grid expands and the green capacity meter grows. At the same time, the red memory-and-time meter also grows. A larger ring dimension can support more slots and, under appropriate security limits, a larger total coefficient modulus. That may permit deeper computation or more precision. However, every polynomial operation, ciphertext, and evaluation key becomes more expensive. Parameter selection is therefore a balance among security, packing, precision, depth, memory, and latency. We do not choose the largest available N automatically. We choose the smallest secure dimension that can support the planned circuit and numerical target.

  Microsoft SEAL represents the coefficient modulus as a product of several primes. The four colored blocks q3 through q0 are the active Residue Number System components at the top of the chain. The rows below show successive data levels with fewer active primes. As the token descends, each row receives a different parameter identity because its modulus set is different. Ciphertexts can be added only when their important metadata is compatible, including level and scale. SEALContext validates the encryption parameters and builds this chain before computation begins. The visual reduction in active blocks is real resource consumption: once a prime has been removed from a ciphertext level, ordinary evaluation does not simply restore it.

  Multiplication changes more than the encrypted numerical value. Both input ciphertexts here carry scale two to the power of forty. When they enter the multiplication gate, the product scale is approximately two to the power of eighty. The red output and the rising gauge make that metadata growth visible. If we continued multiplying without managing scale and modulus, the represented magnitude would approach the available coefficient-modulus ceiling and later operations would become incompatible or inaccurate. This is why C-K-K-S code must inspect scale and chain position rather than treating ciphertexts as opaque numbers. Multiplication increases algebraic degree before relinearization and increases scale before rescaling; both state changes must be handled deliberately.

  Rescaling brings the scale back to a manageable range by dividing by the final active modulus prime. Watch the last prime detach and fall away from the chain. At the same moment, the scale meter contracts from about two to the power of eighty toward two to the power of forty. The ciphertext then moves from level three to level two, and the remaining primes shift together. This operation makes future additions and multiplications easier to align, but it permanently consumes one level and introduces controlled rounding. Rescaling is therefore not a free normalization step. The circuit must contain enough primes for every planned multiplication-and-rescale stage, with suitable bit sizes to preserve the required precision.

  The final diagram checks whether the parameter budget actually fits the circuit. On the left, the ciphertext starts at the top level and descends once after each multiplication. Three multiplication stages therefore require three usable level transitions. On the right, the educational configuration uses polynomial modulus degree 8192, coefficient-modulus bit sizes 60, 40, 40, and 60, and initial scale two to the power of forty. The comparison at the bottom makes the decision explicit: depth needed is three, levels available is three, so the example reports a match. This does not make the configuration universal. A different model, security target, or precision requirement may need another degree, modulus chain, or scale. Parameters must be selected from the planned circuit, not copied blindly."

- **Hoạt ảnh Manim (Visual Actions):**
  1. Selector chạy từ `4096` tới `8192` rồi `16384`; SlotGrid nở theo capacity, trong khi hai meter `capacity` và `memory / time` cùng tăng với tốc độ khác nhau để thể hiện tradeoff.
  2. Bốn prime `q3, q2, q1, q0` tạo thành coefficient modulus. Bên dưới, token đi qua các level có số prime hoạt động giảm dần; mỗi level hiện một parameter identity riêng.
  3. Hai ciphertext mang scale `2^40` cùng đi vào cổng nhân. Output đổi thành `2^80`; scale gauge nằm ở một hàng riêng bên dưới output, kéo dài sát modulus ceiling mà không chạy xuyên qua card.
  4. Prime cuối được chỉ rõ rồi rơi khỏi chain. Cùng lúc, scale co từ `2^80` về `2^40`, nhãn level đổi từ 3 xuống 2 và các prime còn lại dịch vào giữa.
  5. Bốn level bên trái được ghi rõ `start`, `after multiply 1`, `after multiply 2`, `after multiply 3`; token hạ xuống sau mỗi cổng nhân. Bên phải hiện riêng parameter budget, rồi so sánh trực tiếp `depth needed: 3` với `levels available: 3` và chỉ kết luận `MATCH` khi hai phía bằng nhau.
